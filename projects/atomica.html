Atomica

A 2D particle simulator built in C# using MonoGame, because nothing screams ‚Äúproductive weekend‚Äù like simulating virtual dust specks being harassed around by gravity. Atomica lets you visualize basic Newtonian physics in real-time, with very intuitive controls (trust).

Features That Definitely Exist

  • Real-time 2D particle simulation (yep, things move)
  • Change gravity direction with the arrow keys (truly groundbreaking)
  • Toggle cursor-following mode, if you want the particles to chase your mouse like needy toddlers
  • Resizable window support (unless your machine says "no")
  • Built-in instructions rendered with System.Drawing because why not

How It Works

Atomica is powered by MonoGame, the framework of choice for people who enjoy writing their own physics engine instead of using Unity like a normal person. The particles follow basic Newtonian physics, meaning they accelerate due to gravity, bounce off walls, and sometimes just decide to ignore the laws of physics if your code is bad (oops).

Technologies: C#, MonoGame, System.Drawing

Controls

  • Arrow Keys: Change gravity direction. North? South? Your call.
  • Spacebar: Toggle whether the particles follow your cursor. It's like being a wizard, but with fewer consequences.
  • Esc: Quits the application (What more do you want).
